The GeoTIFF Web Page

Last Updated: 11 July, 2000

GeoTIFF represents an effort by over 160 different remote sensing, GIS, cartographic, and surveying related companies and organizations to establish a TIFF based interchange format for georeferenced raster imagery.

Information and Support:

  • GeoTIFF Revision 1.0 Specification: This is the official release version of the GeoTIFF spec.

  • http://www.remotesensing.org/geotiff/geotiff.html: Primary GeoTIFF web site (this page).

  • ftp://ftp.remotesensing.org/pub/geotiff/: Current archive of GeoTIFF spec, EPSG/POSC tables, and libgeotiff source code.

  • ftp://ftp.remotesensing.org/pub/geotiff/samples: Sample GeoTIFF files.

  • ftp://ftpmcmc.cr.usgs.gov/release/geotiff/images/: Various sample GeoTIFF files.

  • GeoTIFF FAQ written by Mike Ruth.

  • Unofficial Catalog of Projections and their parameters.

  • http://gdal.velocet.ca/projects/tiff/gt_index.html: Frank Warmerdam's GeoTIFF notes, and sample files.

Somewhat Out of Date Resources:

  • ftp://www.spot.com/ftp/pub/outgoing/GeoTIFF/: An additional GeoTIFF archive maintained by SPOT Image, USA.

  • ftp://ftpmcmc.cr.usgs.gov/release/geotiff/jpl_mirror/: Old archive of GeoTIFF spec, EPSG/POSC tables, and old libgeotiff source code.

libgeotiff

Libgeotiff is a public domain library normally hosted on top of libtiff for reading, and writing GeoTIFF information tags. Please contact warmerda@home.com for more information, or to get involved.

  • Libgeotiff API: Partial documentation.

  • libgeotiff CVS Web Access

  • libgeotiff.1.1.2.tar.gz or libgeotiff.1.1.2.zip: Released libgeotiff source.

  • ftp://ftp.remotesensing.org/pub/geotiff/libgeotiff/ includes a variety of other items, including pre-built libraries and listgeo executables for various platforms.

  • Bug reports for libgeotiff, the GeoTIFF web pages, or specification can be entered or viewed via BugZilla.

GeoTIFF Mailing List

A mailing-list is currently active to discuss the on-going development of this standard, and the libgeotiff support library. To subscribe to this list, send e-mail to: 
       listproc@mtmorrison.jpl.nasa.gov
with no subject and the body of the message reading: 
     SUBSCRIBE geotiff  Your_FirstName   Your_LastName
To post inquiries directly to the list, send email to: 
       geotiff@mtmorrison.jpl.nasa.gov

A threaded mail-archive of the GeoTIFF mailing-list discussions is online and available.
