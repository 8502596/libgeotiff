GeoTIFF Data Providers

    The following organizations are now or will soon be providing GeoTIFF-compatible raster data products:

    • The USGS is now producing prototypes of their Digital Raster Graphic (DRG) format data; previous versions were supplied only with a Federal Geographic Data Committee compliant Metadata file, and a ".TFW" TIFF world-file, but the latest release have established GeoTIFF as the standard georeferencing method on the CD-ROM. The USGS currently maintains an FTP Archive of GeoTIFF test data, including DRG prototypes.

    • Trifid will be implementing GeoTIFF by mid-December ('95) on all of its commercial data products.

    • GeoSpectrum plans to use GeoTIFF tags to reference radio propagation raster sets. Contact Dan Hawkins for more info.

    • ImageLinks is an image processing service company specializing in high volume, low cost geocoding, mosaicking, tonal balancing and data fusion plus many other processing services of satellite, aerial, map and other data. They guarantee turn-around times of 48 hours or less from receipt of data for their standard products. GeoTiff output is currently in final testing; they will begin shipping products in GeoTiff format beginning in Dec 96.

    • The New York State Dept of Transportation (NYSDOT) is now updating their TIFF Raster Quads with GeoTIFF 1.0 tags. For more info contact mapinfo@dot.state.ny.us. or

    • Space Imaging will offer 1 meter satellite imagery starting later this year (using aerial imagery to simulate their satellite products) and be in full production when they fly towards the end of 1997. The data formats will include GeoTIFF.

    • SPOT intends to start shipping the SPOTView product line in GeoTIFF format starting 1 Jan 1996. (SPOTView is ortho-corrected map-framed imagery in "conventional" grid coordinate systems, such as UTM, State Plane, or National Grid projections.)
