GeoTIFF-Compatible Software

Updated: 22 March, 1996

    The following organizations are now or will soon be providing GeoTIFF-compatible software:

    • Geosoft's OASIS montaj(tm) Version 4.3 supports read and write access to GeoTIFF files with projections enabled.

    • Bentley and HMR. The imaging software MicroStation Descartes and MicroStation ReproGraphics support GeoTIFF starting with v 7.0.

    • Intergraph plans to release MBI (MGE Base Imager) and MAI (MGE Advanced Imager) on both Windows NT and Windows 95' platforms in April 96'. These products run on MicroStation 5.5 or 05.00.95. GeoTIFF read/write capability will also be installed in I/RASC running on NT or Win95', and IV (Image Viewer) running on NT, starting in April '96.

    • ESRI plans on supporting GeoTIFF with the next release of ArcView, Version 3.0. ArcView read the coordinate information held in the GeoTIFF tags and place the image in the correct place. ESRI also plans on supporting the reading of GeoTIFF in the next release of ARC/INFO, Version 7.1. Coordinate information will be read from the GeoTIFF tags and used to place the image in the correct space during display or conversion. Support for the writing to GeoTIFF and full support for the projection meta data is planned for later releases of ARC/INFO.

      Applied Global Technologies (AGT, Inc) has been supporting the USGS DRG effort, and now has made available a freeware version of their DRG/GeoTIFF viewer.

    • ERDAS plans full read/write support for GeoTIFF in the Version 9 of IMAGINE, due out thrid quarter of '96. This includes native viewing and editing capabilities (i.e. no file conversion necessary).

    • PCI's V6.0 EASI/PACE release includes GeoTIFF support for a variety of projections. This release is currently shipping.

      Their PCI Freeware Viewer is now available on their ftp site, providing GeoTIFF read-support. This gives a reference implementation of a GeoTIFF reader that people can use for comparison.

    • ER Mapper will be supporting Geotiff in their next release v. 5.5.

    • SoftDesk provided one of the tags for the GeoTIFF implementation, and has plans to support GeoTIFF in some of their CadOverlay GIS/CAD software.

    • DataDoors will also be implementing GeoTIFF is the upcoming release of DataDoors, first quarter of 1996. DataDoors is a satellite and digital orthophoto access and delivery system which facilitates quick and simple acquisition of imagery especially for lay users. It functions both on the Web or on a CD (or stack of CDs).

    • Laser-Scan reports the following:
      "Laser-Scan has been closely following the development of the GeoTIFF standard, and plans to include support for GeoTIFF in future releases of its product range during 1996. This includes the Gothic ADE object-oriented spatial Application Development Environment, the LAMPS and LAMPS2 Mapping and Charting products, and the IGIS remote sensing Integrated GIS products."

    • Innovativ Vision (IV) will provide support for GeoTiff in the TIP family of remote sensing and image production systems, and for DocEye image archive systems. For more information on these products, contact Kenneth Lundgren or Fredrik Lundh.

    • Python Imaging Library:

      Fredrik Lundh (above) also reports:

      Full GeoTIFF support is planned for the (yet to be released) Python Imaging Library. It will not be included in the first release (slated for early March), but hopefully soon thereafter.

      The Python scripting environment runs on almost every existing platform, it is already available as a Netscape plugin, and an OLE Script version for Windows/Mac is underway (allowing you to transparently plug it into any OLE compatible application).

    • Able Software is in the process of adding support to GeoTIFF in their R2V for Windows software; support will appear in their next major release, scheduled for 1996. Able software is a provider of advanced Raster to Vector Conversion Systems for Mapping and GIS.

    • MapInfo Corporation provides GeoTIFF importing capability with their V 4.0 release of MapInfo (Shipped December '95).

    • MicroImages will be supporting GeoTIFF in their TNTMips, TNTView and TNT Atlas products. Release was scheduled for March 1996.

    • BBNhas plans to implement GeoTIFF readers for some of their DoD customers. They provide information systems to (among others) the logistics and transportation communities.
